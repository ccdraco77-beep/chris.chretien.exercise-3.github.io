A Very Brief History of Cinema

Cinema began in the 1890s with pioneers like Edison (Kinetoscope) and the Lumière brothers, who held the first public, projected screening in 1895. It evolved from short, silent trick films (Méliès) to narrative storytelling, sound (1927), color (1930s), and the blockbuster era, culminating in modern CGI, digital technology, and widespread streaming.
