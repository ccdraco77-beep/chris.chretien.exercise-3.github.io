Deborah Ann Woll

Early Life

Deborah Ann Woll was born on February 7, 1985 in Brooklyn, New York, United States and is an American actress. Her father is an architect, and her mother, Cathy Woll, is a teacher at the Berkeley Carroll School. She is of Irish and German descent. Woll attended the Packer Collegiate Institute, the USC School of Theatre—where she received a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in 2007 and the Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts in London.

Filmography

Woll began her career with guest starring roles in several television series, including Life (2007), ER (2008), CSI: Crime Scene Investigation (2008), My Name Is Earl (2008), and The Mentalist (2008), and had a supporting role in the action-adventure television film Aces 'N' Eights (2008). In 2008, Woll landed her breakout role as Bill Compton's vampire progeny, Jessica Hamby, in the HBO fantasy drama series True Blood. Some other popular movies or TV series she was cast in include:

  • Escape Room, where she plays the role of an Iraq War veteran sent to navigate a series of deadly escape rooms.
  • Daredevil: Born Again, where she plays the role of Karen Page alongside Charlie Cox.
  • Tales From Woodcreek, where she runs an unscripted D&D actual-play as the Dungeon Master for a stellar cast.

Dungeons and Dragons Table Top Gaming

Deborah is an enthusiastic Dungeons & Dragons player and has been interviewed by D&D Beyond and Dragon Talk. She has appeared in several Dungeons & Dragons charity specials, as both a player and a Dungeon Master, produced by Lost Odyssey Events. Woll has appeared as a guest player in Critical Role's second campaign, The Might Nein, where she played a character called Twiggy, an arcane trickster rogue. Her most current endeavor is Tales of Woodcreek, a web-series that she co-created where she is the Dungeon Master for live action play D&D with a stellar cast of celebrities.
